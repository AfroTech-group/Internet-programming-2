Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about Habesha Events

Habesha Events is Ethiopia's premier platform for discovering, attending, and hosting events of all types. From traditional cultural festivals and music concerts to business workshops and community gatherings, we connect people with amazing Ethiopian experiences.

Yes, browsing and attending events is completely free for users. Event organizers pay a small service fee only when they sell tickets through our platform. There are no subscription fees or hidden charges for attendees.

You can create an account by clicking the "Sign Up" button in the top navigation. You'll need to provide your name, email address, and create a password. Alternatively, you can sign up using your Google or Facebook account for faster registration.

You can search for events using the search bar on the Events page. Filter by Ethiopian cities (Addis Ababa, Bahir Dar, Gondar, Mekelle, etc.), category, date, price range, and more. You can also browse events by Ethiopian cultural categories or use the advanced search filters.

To purchase tickets, find an event you're interested in (like Choke Music Fest or Shega Events) and click the "Get Ticket" button. Select the number of tickets you want, review the total, and proceed to checkout. You can pay via mobile banking (helloCash, CBE Birr), credit card, or other local payment methods.

Yes! We support all major Ethiopian mobile banking services including helloCash, CBE Birr, M-Birr, and Amole. During checkout, simply select "Mobile Banking" and follow the instructions for your preferred service.

Refund policies are set by the event organizer. You can check the refund policy on the event page before purchasing. For events like Choke Music Fest or Ethiopian cultural festivals, organizers typically offer refunds up to 7 days before the event date.

After purchasing tickets, you'll receive a confirmation email with your tickets attached as a PDF. You can also access your tickets anytime by logging into your Habesha Events account and going to "My Tickets" in your dashboard. For Ethiopian events, you can also show the QR code on your phone at the entrance.

To post an event, click on "Post Event" in the navigation menu. You'll be guided through a step-by-step process to add event details in Amharic or English, set ticket prices in ETB, upload images, and specify location details. Your event will be reviewed and published within 24 hours.

Habesha Events charges a service fee of 3-5% per ticket sold for paid events, depending on the event size. For free events, there are no fees. There are no upfront costs or subscription fees to list your event on our platform.

Once your event is live, you can access the organizer dashboard to manage ticket sales in ETB, view attendee lists, send SMS/email updates to attendees in Amharic or English, and access analytics about your event performance.

Payments are processed automatically after your event ends. Funds in ETB are typically transferred to your Ethiopian bank account within 3-5 business days after the event completion date. You can set up your payment information in the organizer dashboard.

First, try refreshing the page. If that doesn't work, clear your browser cache and cookies. Make sure you're using an updated browser. For users in Ethiopia with slower connections, try our mobile app which is optimized for local networks.

Event images must be in JPG, PNG, or GIF format and under 5MB in size. Make sure your image meets these requirements. If you're in an area with slow internet, try compressing your image or using our mobile app for easier uploads.

Use the "Cultural Events" filter on our Events page to discover traditional Ethiopian music, dance, food festivals, and cultural celebrations happening across Ethiopia. You can also filter by Ethiopian holidays like Timket, Meskel, and Enkutatash.

We accept all major Ethiopian payment methods: mobile banking (helloCash, CBE Birr, M-Birr, Amole), Ethiopian credit/debit cards, bank transfers, and cash payments at designated locations in major cities.
